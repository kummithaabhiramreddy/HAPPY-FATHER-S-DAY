My Letter
to Dad

Words that could never be enough — but come from the deepest place within me.

Father's Day, June 2025
Dear Dad,

There are days I sit in silence and think about everything you have done for me — and I am completely overwhelmed. Not by sadness, but by a gratitude so immense it brings tears to my eyes. You are the reason I am who I am today.

I watch you sometimes — in the early morning before the house wakes up, sitting quietly with your , shoulders carrying the weight of everything — and I think to myself: this man never asks for anything. He gives and gives and gives, with no expectation, high patiences, no score being kept. You give because that is simply who you are.Entier village will good to see you with you honesty

When I was young, I thought superheroes only lived in comic books. Then I got older and I realized I had been living with one all along. You are my Superman, my Iron Man, my everything — but better, because you are real and you are mine.

I know I haven't always been easy. There were arguments, there were times I thought I knew better, times I was too proud or too young to say "I was wrong and you were right." But you never held it against me. You loved me through every season — the bright ones and the dark ones — without ever making me feel like I had to earn it.

I want you to know something, Dad: every dream I chase, every risk I take, every time I get back up after falling — that's you in me. Your voice is the voice in my head that says "keep going." Your hands are the ones I feel on my back when I need a push. You are present in everything good that I do.

So on this Father's Day, I just want to say what I say too rarely and feel every single day: I love you. Not just today. Not just infront of you. Every day. Every hour. With every breath I take, I am grateful you are my father.

Thank you for being a part of you & extraordinary while making it look ordinary. Thank you for loving me more than I deserved. Thank you for being my Dad.

Your Son 💛
With all the love in the world

II

I Will Always Come Back to You

No matter where life takes me, you will always be home. I will never be too busy or too far to call, to visit, to sit beside you and just be your Son.

III

I Will Take Care of You

Just as you cared for me through every stumble and fall, I will be there for you. Your comfort, your happiness, your peace — they are my responsibility and my honour.

IV

I Will Never Stop Loving You

My love for you is not seasonal or circumstantial. It is permanent, unconditional, and the most certain thing about me. I love you, Dad. Always.
